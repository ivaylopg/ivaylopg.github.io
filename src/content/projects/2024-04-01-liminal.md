Liminal was Pierre Huyghe's major solo exhibition at the 2024 Venice Biennale — a museum-wide installation in which fifteen networked artworks formed a single interconnected system, responsive to each other and to live external data streams. Four of these works were new commissions, each nonlinear or interactive, developed from the ground up for the exhibition.

I served as Technical Director across the full project, from early development in early 2023 through the Venice opening in April 2024. The scope was unusually broad: I led a team of eleven developers and animators working across Unreal Engine, TouchDesigner, and Python, while simultaneously managing the practical realities of a multi-week installation in Venice — where every piece of equipment arrives by boat and logistics become their own creative constraint.

Among the new works, I led development of a self-editing film with a TouchDesigner backend that drove both the editing system during production and the live playback in the exhibition. I also led development of a set of wearable masks equipped with computer vision: each mask catalogued its surroundings in real time and generated a fictional language based on what it observed. Across the exhibition, I worked with the AV vendor to design and implement centralized control systems that allowed the full fifteen-work network to be monitored and maintained as a unified whole.
