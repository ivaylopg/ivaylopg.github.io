Shrine Oberon is a permanent LED installation at the restaurant bar of the New Museum in New York City, created by artist Ian Cheng. At its center is an AI-driven autonomous character — Oberon — who inhabits a richly detailed virtual world, tending to objects and pursuing his own internal logic. Through hidden cameras embedded in the space, he can see the visitors at the bar, recognizing returning guests and quietly acknowledging their presence.

As Technical Producer, I oversaw a distributed team of six developers and artists working across multiple technology stacks — Python AI developers, Unity artists, and AV designers — coordinating parallel workstreams toward a single integrated system. I served as the primary technical liaison between construction vendors, exhibition designers, AV integrators, and software contractors, translating physical constraints of the space into technical requirements and back again.

On the infrastructure side, I developed an on-premises AI system and a C# tools backend that exposes Unity behaviors directly to local language models, eliminating cloud dependencies and ongoing per-use charges. I also designed, prototyped, and oversaw manufacturing for the custom sensor enclosures that allow Oberon to perceive his environment.
