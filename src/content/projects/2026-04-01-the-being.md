The Being is a work by artist Marco Perego in which a neural network, pushed far beyond its limits, endlessly attempts to generate a coherent human face on a large LED wall. The system never quite succeeds — producing instead a continuous, hallucinatory stream of near-faces — while custom furniture in the space quietly measures visitors' breath and heart rates, feeding their presence back into the output.

I came on as Technical Director across two distinct phases: a research period through late 2023 and into 2024, followed by a full production phase culminating in a showing at Centre Pompidou-Metz in early 2025, and subsequently at Jeffrey Deitch in Los Angeles in early 2026.

During research, I evaluated a range of non-contact biometric sensors to identify the right approach for capturing live audience presence without disrupting the experience. I then worked closely with the exhibition designer and sound engineer to design the custom furniture that would house the final sensors — subwoofer transducers, heart rate monitors, and breath detectors — integrated into forms that felt like part of the artwork rather than technology. The production phase brought a tight installation timeline with the LED vendor, sound technicians, and museum staff all converging simultaneously; I directed that process under significant external time pressure. The media server and control system — built in TouchDesigner — drove and modulated the live GAN in response to whatever the room was feeling.
