_Ryan Waiting_ is a work by artist Ryan Gander in which a hyper-realistic digital avatar of the artist is designed to wait — not for an hour, not for a day, but for one hundred years. Accessible through a VR headset or a surrounding halo of monitor screens, the avatar simply exists: idle, present, alive. At its core is an AI brain that self-writes in real time, generating each decision — a sigh, a shift of weight, a glance into the distance — continuously and without interruption, whether or not anyone is watching.

That hundred-year ambition introduced a second, harder problem: longevity. The R&D centred not just on believability but on survival — favouring foundational infrastructure over whatever happens to be technically fashionable today, in the hope that the architecture would still be reliable decades from now. Questions of digital preservation and long-term stewardship shaped every decision about how the work was built.

I served as Technical Producer on the project, produced by Pixels Pixels in collaboration with VIVE Arts. My role was to translate Gander's precise creative vision into a workable production structure — coordinating animators, modellers, riggers, and pipeline engineers working in parallel, and serving as the primary point of contact between the production team and the broader exhibition infrastructure. The brief from VIVE was to find someone who could ask the right questions at the intersection of artistic ambition and long-term technical complexity; that framing became the throughline of my involvement from early R&D through to final delivery.
