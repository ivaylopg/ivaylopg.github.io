Ryan Waiting is a work by artist Ryan Gander in which a hyper-realistic digital avatar of the artist — motion-captured and painstakingly modelled — occupies a VR headset experience alongside a companion monitor display. The avatar simply waits: idle, present, quietly inhabiting the virtual space with the same weight and ambiguity as a person in a room. The work asks what it means to be kept waiting by someone who is, in a sense, always available.

I served as Technical Producer for the project, produced by Pixels Pixels in collaboration with VIVE Arts. My work spanned both production management and technical oversight: coordinating a team of animators, modellers, riggers, and pipeline engineers working in parallel, and serving as the primary point of contact between the production team and the broader exhibition infrastructure. The real-time character was built in a game engine pipeline, with motion capture sessions informing the avatar's behavioural range, and the final experience delivered as a standalone VR installation.
